# The meaning of Allah’s nearness to His servants<br>
18th of Shaʿbān — corresponding to 15 November 2000

## Question:
Allāh—*Mighty and Exalted*—says in the Qur’ān:

**“The angels and the Spirit [i.e., Gabriel] will ascend to Him during a Day the extent of which is fifty thousand years.”**<br>
(al-Maʿārij: 4)

Does this indicate that Allāh controls worldly affairs while He is seated (established) upon the Throne? And if so, how is it that Allāh is closer to us than our jugular vein?

## Answer
All praise is due to Allah. It is established by the Qur’an, the Sunnah, and the consensus of the Salaf of this Ummah that Allāh—*Glorified and Exalted*—is above His heavens, over His Throne; that He is the Most High, the Most Exalted; that He is above everything, and that nothing is above Him.
Allāh the Exalted says:

**“It is Allāh who created the heavens and the earth and whatever is between them in six days; then He established Himself above the Throne. You have not besides Him any protector or any intercessor; so will you not be reminded?”**<br>
(as-Sajdah: 4)

And He says:

**“Indeed, your Lord is Allāh, who created the heavens and the earth in six days and then established Himself above the Throne arranging the matter [of His creation]. ”** <br>
(Yūnus: 3)

And He says:

**“To Him ascends good speech, and righteous work raises it.”**<br>
(Fāṭir: 10)

And He says:

**“He is the First (nothing is before Him) and the Last (nothing is after Him), the Most High (nothing is above Him) and the Most Near (nothing is nearer than Him).”**<br>
(al-Ḥadīd: 3)

And the Prophet ﷺ said:

**“You are the Most High, so nothing is above You.”**<br>
(Sunan al-Tirmidhī: 3481)

The verses and narrations conveying this meaning are many.

Despite this, Allāh—*Glorified be He*—has also informed us that He is with His servants wherever they may be. Allāh says:

**“ Have you not seen that Allāh knows whatsoever is in the heavens and whatsoever is on the earth? There is no Najwā (secret counsel) of three but He is their fourth (with His Knowledge, while He Himself is over the Throne, over the seventh heaven), nor of five but He is their sixth (with His Knowledge), nor of less than that or more but He is with them (with His Knowledge) wheresoever they may be.”**<br>
(al-Mujādilah: 7)

Indeed, Allah has combined the mention of His being above the Throne and His being with His servants in a single verse. He says:

**“He it is Who created the heavens and the earth in six Days and then rose over (Istawā) the Throne (in a manner that suits His Majesty). He knows what goes into the earth and what comes forth from it, and what descends from the heaven and what ascends thereto. And He is with you (by His Knowledge) wheresoever you may be.”**<br>
(al-Ḥadīd: 4)

The meaning of His being *“with”* us is not that He is mixed with the creation. Rather, He is with His servants by His knowledge, while He is above His Throne, and nothing of their deeds is hidden from Him.

As for His saying:

**“And We are closer to him than his jugular vein.”**<br>
(Qāf: 16)

The majority of the commentators said that what is meant is His closeness—*Glorified be He*—through His angels who are entrusted with recording the deeds of the servants. And those who said, “What is meant is Allāh Himself,” explained it as closeness through His knowledge, just as the maʿiyyah (being with) is explained. This is the doctrine of Ahl al-Sunnah wa’l-Jamāʿah: they affirm Allāh’s exaltedness above His creation and His being with His servants, and they declare Him—*Exalted be He*—free from indwelling within created things. 

As for the negators (such as the Jahmiyyah and those who followed them), they deny His being above the creation in His essence and His establishment over the Throne, and they claim that He is present in every place. We ask Allāh the Exalted to grant guidance to us and to the Muslims.


ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Nāṣir al-Barrāk
