# ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Nāṣir al-Barrāk

## Biography:

### His Name and Lineage:

ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Nāṣir al-Barrāk. His lineage traces back to the clan of Āl ʿUraynah, a branch of the Subayʿ tribe, which belongs to the Muḍarī Adnānī Arab tribes.

### His Birth and Upbringing

The Shaykh was born in the month of Dhū al-Qaʿdah in the year 1352 AH in al-Bukayriyyah, one of the cities of the Qassim region.

His father passed away when he was only one year old, so he did not know him. His mother took responsibility for raising him and raised him with the finest upbringing. She was a righteous and virtuous woman. During his childhood, he grew up in the home of his maternal uncles.

When he reached the age of five, he traveled with his mother to Makkah, where he came under the guardianship of his stepfather Muḥammad ibn Ḥamūd al-Barrāk.

In Makkah, the Shaykh enrolled in al-Raḥmāniyyah School while in the second year of primary education. Allah decreed that he be afflicted with an illness in his eyes which resulted in the loss of his eyesight when he was nine years old.

### His Pursuit of Knowledge and His Teachers

He returned from Makkah to al-Bukayriyyah with his family, where he memorized the Qur’an at around the age of ten under his uncle ʿAbd Allāh ibn Manṣūr al-Barrāk, then recited to the town’s Qur’an reciter ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Sālim al-Kridīs, may Allah have mercy on them all.

Around the years 1364–1365 AH, the Shaykh began attending lessons and studying under scholars. He studied portions of Kitāb al-Tawḥīd and al-Ājurrūmiyyah with Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn ʿAbd Allāh al-Subayyil, judge of al-Bukayriyyah, al-Khabrāʾ, and al-Badāʾiʿ, after his teacher Ibn Muqbil (may Allah have mercy on him). He also studied al-Uṣūl al-Thalāthah with Shaykh Muḥammad ibn Muqbil, judge of al-Bukayriyyah.

He then traveled once more to Makkah around 1366 AH, where he stayed for three years. There he studied al-Ājurrūmiyyah with Shaykh ʿAbd Allāh ibn Muḥammad al-Khalīfī, the imam of al-Masjid al-Ḥarām. During this period, he met a distinguished scholar and one of the senior students of the eminent scholar Muḥammad ibn Ibrāhīm, namely Shaykh Ṣāliḥ ibn Ḥusayn al-ʿAlī al-ʿIrāqī, may Allah have mercy on him. He was a close companion of Imam ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Bāz, may Allah have mercy on him. The Shaykh studied with him Tawḥīd, ʿAqīdah, inheritance law, and Arabic grammar.

When Shaykh Ṣāliḥ al-ʿIrāqī was appointed director of al-ʿAzīziyyah School in the town of al-Dilam, he wished for Shaykh ʿAbd al-Raḥmān to accompany him out of esteem and care for him. Thus, he accompanied him to seek knowledge from Shaykh Ibn Bāz, who at that time was serving as a judge in al-Dilam. He traveled with him in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 1369 AH, enrolled in al-ʿAzīziyyah School in the fourth grade, and benefited greatly that year, particularly by acquiring the foundational rules of tajwīd.

That same year, he traveled for ḥajj with a group of students alongside Shaykh Ibn Bāz. After returning, he left formal schooling at al-ʿAzīziyyah and instead devoted himself to memorizing foundational texts with the students of Shaykh Ibn Bāz and attending his various lessons. Among the books studied with him were: Kitāb al-Tawḥīd, al-Uṣūl al-Thalāthah, ʿUmdat al-Aḥkām, Bulūgh al-Marām, Musnad Aḥmad, Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr, al-Raḥbiyyah, and al-Ājurrūmiyyah.

He remained in al-Dilam under the care of Shaykh al-ʿIrāqī, residing in his home, and studied ʿIlm al-ʿArūḍ (Arabic prosody) with him. During this period, he memorized Kitāb al-Tawḥīd, al-Uṣūl al-Thalāthah, al-Ājurrūmiyyah, Qaṭr al-Nadā, Naẓm al-Raḥbiyyah, portions of Alfiyyat Ibn Mālik, and Alfiyyat al-ʿIrāqī in ḥadīth sciences. He remained in al-Dilam until the end of 1370 AH, a period that had a profound impact on his scholarly life.

### His Studies at the Institute and the College

The Shaykh enrolled in the Scientific Institute in Riyadh upon its opening on 1/1/1371 AH. The program lasted four years, and he graduated in the first cohort in 1374 AH. He then enrolled in the College of Sharīʿah, graduating in 1378 AH.

He studied under many scholars, among the most prominent of them:

Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Bāz

Shaykh Muḥammad al-Amīn al-Shanqīṭī, may Allah have mercy on them both
who taught tafsīr and uṣūl al-fiqh.

He also studied with Shaykh ʿAbd al-Razzāq ʿAfīfī, may Allah have mercy on him, who taught Tawḥīd, Arabic grammar, and uṣūl al-fiqh, as well as Shaykh Muḥammad ʿAbd al-Razzāq Ḥamzah, Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Nāṣir al-Rashīd, Shaykh ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Afrīqī, Shaykh ʿAbd al-Laṭīf Sarḥān (with whom he studied grammar), Shaykh ʿAbd Allāh ibn Ṣāliḥ al-Khalīfī, and others—may Allah have mercy on them all.

During this time, he also attended some lessons of Shaykh Muḥammad ibn Ibrāhīm Āl al-Shaykh in the mosque.

The greatest of his teachers and the one who had the most profound influence on him was Imam ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Bāz, may Allah have mercy on him. He benefited from him for over fifty years, from 1369 AH until his death in 1420 AH. He also greatly benefited from Shaykh al-ʿIrāqī, from whom he learned love for evidence, rejection of blind imitation, and precision in the sciences of the Arabic language such as grammar, morphology, and prosody.

### The Shaykh and Teaching

The Shaykh was nominated for a judicial position by order of the Chief Judge of his time, Shaykh Muḥammad ibn Ibrāhīm Āl al-Shaykh, but he declined through the mediation of Shaykh ʿAbd al-Laṭīf ibn Ibrāhīm Āl al-Shaykh, who later selected him for teaching in the scientific institutes.

Shaykh ʿAbd al-Raḥmān officially taught at the Riyadh Institute from 1379–1381 AH, then moved to the College of Sharīʿah in 1382 AH, and later to the College of Uṣūl al-Dīn after its establishment in 1395 AH, where he was assigned to the Department of Creed and Contemporary Sects. He continued teaching in both colleges until his retirement in 1420 AH, supervising dozens of academic theses.

Despite the college’s desire to contract him afterward, he declined, preferring to teach in his mosque. Shaykh Ibn Bāz repeatedly requested that he take up issuing fatwā, but he declined. He only agreed, out of modesty, to substitute during the summer in Dār al-Iftāʾ for two summers, after which he stopped.

After the death of Shaykh Ibn Bāz, the Grand Mufti Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz Āl al-Shaykh renewed the request that he join the council of fatwā, but he again declined, preferring seclusion for teaching in his home and mosques.

He began holding regular lessons in the mosque adjacent to his first home from 1381 AH, when he was appointed its imam. Later, upon moving to his second home in al-Fārūq district, east of al-Malaz, in 1403 AH, he was appointed imam of al-Khalīfī Mosque, where he continues teaching to this day, may Allah grant him long life.

He also delivers lessons in several other mosques across Riyadh and gives lectures throughout the Kingdom, either in person or by phone. His weekly lessons exceed twenty across various Islamic sciences.

He is distinguished by teaching books—both concise and extensive—in nearly all fields: creed, tafsīr, ḥadīth, Arabic language sciences, and others. He also teaches logic to some students in his home. Most of his students are university professors, callers to Islam, and teachers, all of whom hold him in high esteem and love him deeply.

### His Scholarly Output

The Shaykh is known for his asceticism regarding fame and his humility. Despite possessing the tools of authorship—vast knowledge, memorization of evidences, intellectual clarity—he refrained from writing. Most of his published works originated as oral lessons that were transcribed, reviewed with him, and then published. They sufficiently demonstrate his knowledge and scholarly standing. His unpublished audio lessons and lectures far exceed what has been published, in addition to his carefully reasoned fatāwā based on evidence, legal reasoning, consideration of objectives of the Sharīʿah, and differing contexts. Many of these are published on his official website.
