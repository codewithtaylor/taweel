# ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz bin Marzūq aṭ-Ṭurayfī

## Biography

### Birth and Early Life

He was born in Kuwait in 1396 AH due to his father’s work there. His father later returned to Saudi Arabia before Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz reached the age of discernment.

He completed his university studies at the College of Sharia at **Imam Muhammad ibn Saud Islamic University** in Riyadh.

### Academic Formation

He began memorizing foundational scholarly texts at the age of thirteen. The first text he memorized was *al-Bayqūniyyah* in Hadith terminology.

At the age of fifteen, he began intensive reading, memorization, and study. During this period, he read dozens of volumes, including:

- *Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr*
- *Zād al-Maʿād*
- *Sīrat Ibn Hishām*
- *Fatāwā Ibn Taymiyyah* (first reading)

He also attended lessons sporadically around the time of puberty and shortly thereafter.

He attended only a few lessons weekly (around three), while dedicating most of his time to reading and summarizing books—without restricting himself to a single discipline. His reading spanned:

- Creed (*ʿaqīdah*)
- Sunnah
- Fiqh
- Biography (*sīrah*)
- History
- Language, poetry, and literature

Summarizing lengthy works benefited him greatly. At the ages of sixteen and seventeen, he personally abridged:

- *Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr* (in its entirety)
- *Zād al-Maʿād*
- Parts of *al-Mughnī*
- Parts of *Fatāwā Ibn Taymiyyah*

He also summarized nearly half of *al-Istidhkār* by Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr, carefully organizing its legal issues and memorizing its poetic evidences. He compiled an index of Ibn Taymiyyah’s fatwas, extracting legal and hadith principles, as well as references to scholars, places, and legal schools.

By puberty and shortly thereafter, he had memorized many foundational texts, including:

- *al-Uṣūl al-Thalāthah*
- *Kashf al-Shubuhāt*
- *Kitāb al-Tawḥīd*
- *Faḍl al-Islām*
- *al-Manẓūmah al-Raḥbiyyah*
- *Bulūgh al-Marām*

He also memorized hundreds of lines of poetry. All of this was completed by the age of eighteen.

In that year, he began studying *Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī*, then *Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim*, followed by *Sunan Abī Dāwūd* and the remaining primary Hadith collections.

He began memorizing the legal discussions from al-Zarkashī’s commentary on *Mukhtaṣar al-Khiraqī* (seven volumes) in the Hanbali school, up to the Book of Prayer, then shifted to memorizing the transmitted narrations of the madhhab.

He also mastered the legal issues and evidences of *Manār al-Sabīl* by Ibn Ḍuwayyān.

Likewise, he studied *al-Risālah* by Ibn Abī Zayd al-Qayrawānī in Mālikī fiqh and read approximately five of its commentaries.

### Memorization of Hadith

He memorized Hadith through repetition, typically **30–50 hadiths per day**, sometimes more or less.

Regarding chains of narration:

- If the hadith was authentic, he memorized the principal chain.
- If it was defective or problematic, he generally memorized all its routes of transmission.


### Scholarly Reading

#### Books of Sunnah

He read most of the major works of Sunnah, including:

- *Sunan al-Bayhaqī*
- *Sunan Saʿīd ibn Manṣūr*
- *Ṣaḥīḥ Ibn Khuzaymah*
- *Ṣaḥīḥ Ibn Ḥibbān*
- The *Muṣannafs* of Ibn Abī Shaybah and ʿAbd al-Razzāq (more than once)
- *al-Muḥallā* by Ibn Ḥazm (several times)
- *al-Awsat* by Ibn al-Mundhir
- *Maʿrifat al-Sunan wa al-Āthār* by al-Bayhaqī
- *Sunan al-Dāraquṭnī*
- *al-Maṭālib al-ʿĀliyah*

Along with many individual hadith collections, with particular attention to the jurisprudence of the Salaf.

#### Books of ʿIlal (Hidden Defects)

- *Sharḥ ʿIlal al-Tirmidhī* by Ibn Rajab
- *ʿIlal* of:
  - al-Dāraquṭnī
  - Ibn Abī Ḥātim
  - Ibn al-Madīnī
  - Ibn Maʿīn
  - al-Khallāl
  - Imam Ahmad

#### Fiqh

**Mālikī:**
- *al-Tamhīd*
- *al-Istidhkār*
- *al-Mudawwanah*
- *al-Muqaddimāt*, and others

**Ḥanafī:**
- *al-Ḥujjah ʿalā Ahl al-Madīnah*
- *al-Āthār* of Abū Yūsuf and Muḥammad ibn al-Ḥasan
- *Ḥāshiyat Ibn ʿĀbidīn*

**Ḥanbalī:**
- Nearly all narrations of Imam Ahmad
- The *Masāʾil* collections
- *Sharḥ al-Zarkashī*
- *al-Sharḥ al-Kabīr*
- *Kashshāf al-Qināʿ*
- *Manār al-Sabīl* (repeatedly)

**Shāfiʿī:**
- *al-Umm*
- *al-Majmūʿ*
- *al-Sharḥ al-Kabīr*

#### Tafsīr

- Transmitted tafsīrs such as:
  - Ibn Jarīr
  - Ibn Abī Ḥātim
  - al-Baghawī
- *Tafsīr Ibn al-Mundhir* (read in manuscript before publication)
- *Tafsīr ʿAbd ibn Ḥumayd* (read in manuscript)
- He abridged *Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr*
- Read portions of:
  - al-Qurṭubī
  - Ibn ʿAṭiyyah
  - al-Zamakhsharī
  - al-Thaʿlabī

#### History

- Books of biographical classes (*ṭabaqāt*)
- *Tārīkh al-Islām* by al-Dhahabī
- Portions of *Shadharāt al-Dhahab*
- *Tārīkh Dimashq* by Ibn ʿAsākir
- *al-Bidāyah wa al-Nihāyah*

### Teachers

- Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz ibn Bāz — attended his lessons for approximately four years
- Shaykh Muḥammad ʿAbd Allāh al-Ṣūmālī al-Makkī (رحمه الله)
- Ustadh Ḥasan al-Athīyūbī — Arabic grammar and morphology
- Dr. Muḥammad Ajmal al-Iṣlāḥī — linguist and literary scholar
- Shaykh Ṣafī al-Raḥmān al-Mubārakfūrī (رحمه الله)
- Shaykh Muḥammad al-Barnī al-Hindī — *Fatḥ al-Bārī*
- Shaykh ʿAbd Allāh ibn ʿAqīl
- Shaykh ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Barrāk — *al-Iʿtiqād* of al-Bayhaqī
- Shaykh ʿAbd al-Karīm al-Khuḍayr — *al-Bāʿith al-Ḥathīth* (1418 AH)
- Shaykh Ṣāliḥ Āl al-Shaykh — *Mufradāt Gharīb al-Qurʾān* and *Sunan Abī Dāwūd*

### Lessons and Teaching

He is distinguished for his mastery of the Sunnah and continuous engagement with it. He has delivered lessons in Riyadh on:

- *Aḥādīth al-Aḥkām al-Ṣughrā*
- *al-Arbaʿīn al-Nawawiyyah*
- *Nukhbat al-Fikar*
- *al-Muḥarrar*
- *Lumʿat al-Iʿtiqād*
- *Kitāb al-Tawḥīd*

### Notes

1. al-Suyūṭī states in *al-Ansāb* that *al-Ṭurayfī* (with both vowelings) is a clan from the tribe of Ṭayy.
